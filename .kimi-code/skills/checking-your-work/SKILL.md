# Checking Your Work

## What this is for

Once you've built something, the best way to know it's good is to look at it and try it. Weakerpowers doesn't ask beginners to write automated tests — instead, you and the person check the work with your own eyes and hands. This teaches the most important habit in building things: trust what you see, and try the thing the way a real person would.

**Say at the start:** "Let's look over what we made and try it out."

## How to check together

### 1. Look at it
Open the thing — the web page, the file, whatever you built — and look at it together. Walk through it out loud: "Here's the heading we added, here are your three sections, and here's the button at the bottom."

### 2. Try it like a real person would
Use it the way someone actually would. Click the buttons. Type in the boxes. Open it on the screen it's meant for. Ask the person to try it too — it's *their* thing, and they'll notice things you won't.

> "Go ahead and click around. Does it do what you pictured?"

### 3. Compare it to what you wanted
Look back at the plan and the feeling you were going for. Does it match? Walk through the "what it'll do" list from the plan and confirm each thing actually happens.

### 4. Notice what feels off
If something looks wrong or feels clunky, that's worth fixing. Trust the person's reaction — if they hesitate or frown, ask what's bugging them. Small things matter: a color that's hard to read, a button that's hard to find, a word that's confusing.

## When something needs fixing

Found something? Great — that's the whole point of checking. Fix it the same calm way you built things:

- Figure out the real reason it's off.
- Make the change.
- Look again together to confirm it's better now.

No shame in fixes. Even experienced builders find things to fix when they look closely.

## A gentle checklist

Run through these together before calling it done:

- Does it do the main thing it was supposed to do?
- Did you actually try it, not just look at it?
- Does it feel the way you wanted it to feel?
- Is there anything confusing or hard to use?
- Did the person get to try it themselves?

## When it looks good

Once you've both looked it over and tried it and it does what you wanted:

> "This looks great and it does what we set out to do. Let's save it and figure out what's next."

Then use the wrapping-up skill to save the work and decide what comes next.
